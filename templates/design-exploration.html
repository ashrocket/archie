The Chosen Direction

CHOSEN

Illustrated Storybook RPG

A picture book that plays like an RPG. Hand-drawn _why-style illustrations on living pages with EarthBound dialogue boxes, item-get response delivery, and parallax pastoral scenes.

Core Concept

The page IS the overworld. Archie is a hand-drawn character who lives in a book that behaves like a game. The user is reading a living page — ink illustrations, warm color washes, handwritten marginalia — but the illustrations move, the character walks, you can talk to him, and he gives you things.

DNA Sources

  • _why the lucky stiff: Chunky hand-drawn characters interrupting content. Humor in the margins. Art that's rough on purpose. The feeling that someone made this for you at 2 AM.
  • Harry Potter: The page is alive. Ink moves. Portraits talk back. Maps reveal themselves. The medium itself is magical.
  • EarthBound / RPG: Dialogue boxes with text crawl. Item-get moments. A safe place to be confused. An NPC who notices you're there.

What This Is NOT

  • Not a game — no goals, no progression, no fail states
  • Not pixel art — hand-drawn ink and color wash
  • Not polished/corporate — deliberately rough, handmade, personal
  • Not ironic retro — earnestly recreating warmth
  • Not decoration — the visual experience IS the product

Character Design: Archie

CHOSEN

Archie used to go by Velmo — but that was a while ago. He's Archie now, and Archie fits better. He's investigative, warm, a little nerdy. More cerebral than physical. Masculine but carries a bit of softness — not in a way he hides, just in a way that's his.

Visual Description

  • Hand-drawn, chunky ink lines, rough-edged, charming because it's imperfect
  • 2-3 head proportion (storybook character, not chibi, not realistic)
  • Slightly oversized round glasses — the Velmo DNA: cerebral, searching, owlish
  • A coat he lives in — oversized, rumpled, professorial but not polished
  • Warm autumn palette: burnt orange, deep teal, warm brown, cream
  • Broad shoulders but gentle posture

Idle Animations

  • Walking through scenes, pausing to examine things
  • Adjusting glasses (thinking gesture)
  • Scribbling in a notebook
  • A bird landing on his head unnoticed
  • Glancing at the user every few seconds

Humor Style

Warm, observational, never punching down. Self-aware ("I just spent ten minutes tracing a dependency cycle that was my own suggestion"). Delightful surprise (a paper airplane arrives with a haiku instead of a paragraph).

Pitch 1: Illustrated / Hand-Drawn

ART DIRECTION PITCH
"Only hand-drawn says: here is a thinking person in a quiet place, and you are welcome to sit with him while the work gets done."

Watercolor and ink-wash as the visual language of thought itself. Imprecise, layered, bleeding one idea into the next. Da Vinci's notebooks, Beatrix Potter's lakeside sketches, Miyazaki's image boards.

How It Works

Watercolor backgrounds organized in atmospheric depth layers — diluted indigo treeline, wet-on-wet meadow greens, ink-wash grass blades. Each layer is a PNG with transparency for CSS parallax. Character sprites as ink-wash sprite sheets where the irregular line gives life to even two-frame walk cycles.

Pipeline

Procreate / Clip Studio Paint → layers exported as WebP (80-85 quality) → sprite sheets via TexturePacker with JSON frame maps → scene manifests (JSON) defining layer order, parallax depth, animation triggers.

Honest Downsides

  • Requires a skilled illustrator — specialized hire
  • 500KB-1MB per scene (watercolor textures compress poorly)
  • Iteration is slow — "add a river" means repainting
  • Each new scene/variant requires new painted assets

Pitch 2: Pixel Art

ART DIRECTION PITCH
"Every single pixel is a deliberate decision. There's no hiding behind gradients or blur."

48px Archie sprite with thinking-pose idle, 6 frames, looping. Slight awkwardness built into the medium. Pixel characters walk a little stiffly, and that warmth comes from visible construction.

The Experience

Dithered sky with shifting clouds. 2-3 layers of pixel landscapes scrolling at different rates. Archie at 48x64px, upscaled with image-rendering: pixelated. Fireflies as 2x2px sine-wave sprites. When the scene goes immersive, Archie sits on a log, pulls out a notebook.

Pipeline

Aseprite ($20) → PNG sprite sheets with JSON metadata → Tiled for parallax backgrounds → enforced 32-color palette (Endesga-32). Full scene: sub-megabyte.

Strongest Argument

Most technically feasible. Scales by integer multiples with zero quality loss. Runs at 60fps on a 2018 phone. A full Archie animation set fits in a single 512x512 PNG.

Honest Downsides

  • "Pixel art" can read as unserious to enterprise buyers
  • Requires Celeste-quality execution to avoid RPG Maker defaults
  • Facial expression range is very limited at low resolution
  • Good pixel artists are specialized and hard to find

Pitch 3: Flat Vector / Geometric

ART DIRECTION PITCH
"Archie doesn't see organic mess. He sees tessellated canopies, triangulated trunks, hexagonal leaf clusters."

Monument Valley meets the mind palace. Clean isometric planes. The slight awkwardness of geometric forms trying to represent organic things. A geometric deer is inherently a little awkward. A geometric tree is inherently a little cerebral.

The Experience

Archie's body as a scene graph of rotating polygons — trapezoid torso, circular joints, triangular feet. No sprite sheets needed. Procedural animation: walk adapts to terrain, head tracks elements, scarf responds to wind. The idle immersive state: Archie on an impossible Escher staircase, thinking.

Pipeline

Figma → SVG export → SVGO optimization → Lottie/Bodymovin for complex animations → Canvas Path2D for character. The entire pipeline stays vector from creation to render. A full forest scene: 15-30KB as SVG.

Honest Downsides

  • Flat vector is everywhere — every SaaS landing page
  • Emotional expression ceiling is low (circle face, dot eyes)
  • Without strong art direction, accidentally looks like Notion clip art
  • Procedural character animation front-loads engineering effort

Pitch 4: Photographic / Collage

ART DIRECTION PITCH
"Archie doesn't live in a UI. He lives in a world of physical artifacts — torn kraft paper, woodgrain, pressed leaves."

Real-world textures layered with hand-drawn illustrated overlays. The result feels made. Not rendered. Not generated. Assembled by someone who cares. A response arrives on a torn scrap of paper that drifts into frame.

Pipeline

Flatbed scanner at 600 DPI + stock textures → Photoshop masking → WebP parallax layers → Procreate/Illustrator for overlays → SVG where possible. Typical scene: under 3MB total.

Strongest Argument

Triggers tactile nostalgia. Users feel like they've opened someone's private journal. The slight imperfection of torn edges, organic grain variation, and natural color palettes create psychological safety. You linger. You don't rush.

Honest Downsides

  • Heaviest asset production — every texture must be sourced, scanned, masked
  • Heaviest file weight of all approaches
  • Color consistency across devices is harder with photographs
  • Adding a new environment means new photography, new scans

Wildcard: Living Ink Simulation

WILDCARD
"Ink in water IS thinking made visible. It curls. It bifurcates. It forms tendrils that reach out, hesitate, split apart, find each other again."

A real-time Navier-Stokes fluid solver via WebGPU compute shaders. Everything — Archie, the landscape, the text — is ink dissolving in water. Not a metaphor. An actual physics simulation where pigment particles are the sole rendering primitive.

How Responses Arrive

When Claude processes, a gentle current pulls all dye toward center — the world contracts like narrowing attention. The response arrives as a pressure shockwave. Text writes itself in ink, holds shape briefly (high viscosity zone), then dissolves. The chat sidebar holds permanent text; the fluid holds the memory as residual dye.

Paper airplane:

A tight V-shaped emitter cluster with a velocity jet, trailing dissolving text in its wake. Hits the chat boundary with an ink splash.

Why It's Wild

No one has shipped a production chat interface rendered through fluid simulation. Every session genuinely looks different (chaotic system). Archie's form is perpetually dissolving and being redrawn — a man made of thought, holding shape against entropy.

Honest Downsides

  • Narrow margin between "stunning" and "spilled coffee on my screen"
  • Mobile WebGPU support is incomplete
  • Screen readers can't parse fluid simulation
  • Might feel like an art installation when you just want to ask a question

Branding: Chibi Flash

BRANDING EXPLORATION
"Something someone made for you at 2 AM. Not something a design team A/B tested."

3:5 head-to-body ratio. Enormous asymmetric eyes with thick ink outlines. Rumpled blazer over too-big turtleneck, flat olive and warm cream fills, zero texture. Tiny stilt legs in comically oversized brown oxfords. Carries a rolled-up blueprint he never opens.

The Movement

Parappa flat-paper planes sliding past each other. 4-6 frame animation with that Newgrounds snap — movement is punctuated, not smooth. He bobs between key poses like a paper puppet on a stick. Two-frame leg-swing loop on a fence that is somehow the most endearing thing you've ever seen.

Touchstones

Homestar Runner, Katamari Damacy cutscenes, Parappa the Rapper, Studio 4°C shorts, Newgrounds golden age.

Branding: XKCD / Stick Figure

BRANDING EXPLORATION
"The smartest thing in the room doesn't need to perform sophistication."

Black lines on white. Round head, single tuft of messy hair, round glasses slightly too big and slightly crooked. No fill. No shading. Napkin sketch energy. The emotional bandwidth of a Pixar character from two wobbly circles and a line.

Key Insight

There's a major overlap between Velmo (Archie's former identity) and the XKCD archetype: cerebral, investigative, glasses, slightly awkward, the one who actually solves it. The personality DNA is almost identical. But Archie's soul is Velmo/XKCD — his visual world needs to be richer.

Verdict: Right character. Wrong costume. Eliminated as visual direction.

Branding: Retro Nintendo RPG

BRANDING EXPLORATION
"They made you feel safe enough to be confused."

32x48px sprite — taller than standard NPCs because he matters. Chrono Trigger proportions: big head, round glasses, oversized coat reading as "professor who forgot his umbrella." Muted autumn palette. Idle animation: shifts weight, adjusts glasses, glances at you every 8 seconds.

Dialogue Box

EarthBound-style rounded window. Text crawls character by character with tick-tick-tick. Archie's portrait shifts to "thinking" variant while processing. Bitmap font, not system font. Code blocks render like spell names in Final Fantasy VI.

Responses as Items

"You received: Archie's Analysis" — code review is a scroll, refactor is a crystal, bug fix is a potion, architecture recommendation is a map fragment. Inventory panel becomes conversation history.

The Emotional Register

Not ironic distance. Radical sincerity. This interaction matters. You matter. The problem you're working on is a quest, and quests are worth taking seriously, and you don't have to do them alone.
